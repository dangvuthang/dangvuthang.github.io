IT Work

For this section, I was lucky enough to have an interview with an IT professional. His name is Tung, and he is my brother-in-law. He is a full-stack software engineer at BodiData company, which is a fashion tech company that uses computer vision to visualize the three sizes of a human body to suggest the perfect size clothes for the user. Since his position required him to work on both the frontend and backend, he provides me with a very high-level overview of what his job usually involves. He usually has an interview with an end-user to discuss the project, then depend on the outcome, he may have to come up with a wireframe for it. After that, he will meet up with his stakeholder to design the workflow process. When everything is ready, he will start to design UI/UX for the product, then start building UI with React. When the frontend is ready, he will work on the backend to create an API and connect it with the frontend application. After numerous testing, it will come to the deployment phase. This comes to me as a surprise since I do not think an IT professionals’ job consists of many tasks. Before this, I only think IT professionals mostly do coding, but he told me that communication is of great importance when you want are a developer. Not only do you need to interact with other colleagues to discuss work-related matters such as seeking/giving advice, doing code review, or requesting access to some resources, but you also need to speak with the sale team to gather clients’ need or discuss the vision and progress with your CEO. While coding is the main task of any developer, he said that you would only spend about 50% of your time to code while the other 50% consists of discussions, team building, and the most important thing is self-study to keep yourself up to date with the latest technology. After discussing with him, I believe the most challenging aspect of the IT professional is the ability to understand the real problems behind end-users. We need to distinguish between what the user wants and what the user needs. He also shares with me what he thought was the essence of the IT industry, “In contrast to conventional belief, the most essential activity is not writing code, but problem-detection and problem-solving”.
