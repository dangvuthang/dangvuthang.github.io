IT Techonologies

Since I enjoy web development. I going to describe the two pieces of technology that have helped tremendously for web developers, and I also may integrate them with my own idea project.

The first one I am going to describe is the upcoming trend of BaaS which stands for backend as a service, or some may realize the term serverless. In the past, to build a large-scale application, you need a backend application to build a web server or exposing API as an endpoint for clients to request. However, with services like Firebase backed by Google, or AWS Amplify, 8Base, they are packed with all the features you may need for the backend application. No longer do you have to manually build the authentication system to check login, these services already offer social login. Not only are they more secure, but they also abstract developers from wasting too much time to implement them. Thus, allowing them to focus on the business logic of the application. Besides, BaaS already comes with very exciting features like cloud storage, real-time database, and hosting solutions, which would take a lot of time and effort to build from scratch. Scalability may be something that backend developers may forget when building their application, but with BaaS, they do not have to worry about this matter as the application is automatically scaled according to their needs. It is also said that some BaaS also offer machine learning capabilities to enhance their application UX. For example, because Firebase is backed by Google, it used Google Cloud computing to provide great features like labeling images, recognizing text, and recognizing landmarks for machine learning. As a result of BaaS, it is now so much easier to transition from the frontend developers to full-stack developers. I believe for the upcoming year; it is possible that developers do not have to think about their backend as all and focus their time and energy on solving the real problem. This BaaS is made possible due to the growth of cloud computing. BaaS has a huge impact on both developers, and small businesses as well. Companies do not have to worry about hiring backend teams to build servers, which would reduce the overall cost. They do not have to worry about scaling up their backend infrastructure to meet the demand of their user. The most important aspect is that they will be able to test their application as soon as possible to know whether some improvements will be needed for the frontend. Developers will have an easier time integrating their work with BaaS and do not have to worry about the security and database management aspect. This is not to say that backend developers will be jobless; as a matter of fact, great technologies only abstract you from the coding part. Companies still need them to maintain and configure according to each specific use case. For me, I believe BaaS will help me a lot when I try to build a project to demo during my time studying at RMIT. I can now focus my time and energy to make beautiful UI and build my application logic without worrying too much about the backend server.

Another technology that has been emerged on the website is the natural processing language. It is about giving a computer the ability to understand the speech of a human. To see this technology in action, you can simply use your smartphone whether it is Android or IOS. For Android, if you say, “OK Google”, you will notice the popup showing up that offers assistance, then just say whatever you need. You can find this technology also on the website. For example, going to the TpBank website, you will see that the chatbot will help to answer some questions you may have. Instead of wasting time in the FAQ section, the chatbot will answer all common questions you may have. Maybe after 3 years, natural processing language will be able to understand any languages in the world. As a result, instead of the traditional input like keyboard, mouse, the computer can be instructed by human’s speech alone with high accuracy. NPL is made possible due to machine learning algorithms. NPL has a huge potential considered the benefit it brings. We may longer require human assistance in some common tasks like providing guidance, feedback with the help of the expert system. Customer service jobs might be the most affected by NPL, and all these jobs may be lost due to the rise of NPL. However, with the current level of NPL level, it is still far from accomplishing it. We sometimes still value what other human being said than the machine. As a developer, hopefully in the future, instead of coding on the keyboard, which would lead to some serious health problems like carpal tunnel, I can just tell the computer instead of through speech recognition. However, it would take a long time for NPL to reach that level. For now, in my daily life, I can just use Google Assistant to remind me about my upcoming schedule, or when is my deadline for an assignment.
